## Design Context

### Users
Ambitious business owners and founders — primarily in Malaysia but open to regional/global clients — who are ready to invest in serious brand work. They arrive already convinced they need branding help; the site's job is to confirm Letus is the right studio and make the decision easy. Secondary audience: referrals who arrive warm and need their conviction reinforced quickly.

They browse on both desktop and mobile. Decision-making context is typically research mode — comparing agencies, not impulse purchases.

### Brand Personality
**Volcanic, precise, uncompromising.**

- *Volcanic*: The name "Letus" means eruption in Malay. The brand should carry latent energy — not loud, but charged. Like pressure before a release.
- *Precise*: Artisanal craft, not mass production. Every detail is chosen. Nothing is accidental.
- *Uncompromising*: Bold positioning, clear POV. Letus doesn't try to please everyone — it attracts the right clients by being distinctly itself.

Emotional goal: a prospect should feel *confident and slightly envious* — "I want my brand to feel like this site does."

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme**: Mixed — cream/off-white (`#faf9f6`) hero and content sections; deep charcoal (`#0f0f0f`/`#141414`) for process and footer; bold orange (`#f05a28`) CTA section.
- **Display font**: Unbounded — geometric, assertive, distinctive. Carries the volcanic weight.
- **Body font**: Inter — clean, functional, gets out of the way.
- **Primary accent**: `#f05a28` (Letus orange). Used sparingly — it's the eruption moment.
- **Deep purple** (`#473256`): supporting accent only, never dominant.
- **Signature element**: BrandConstellation — animated triangular constellation canvas. This is the unforgettable thing. It should feel like a living brand mark, not a background texture.

Anti-references: generic SaaS landing pages, purple-gradient AI aesthetics, glassmorphism, centered-hero-everything layouts.

### Design Principles
1. **One thing nobody else has.** The BrandConstellation is that thing — protect it, foreground it, don't let it become wallpaper.
2. **Asymmetry over symmetry.** Left-anchored layouts, editorial grids, intentional whitespace on one side. Safe centered compositions belong to a different kind of agency.
3. **Copy earns its space.** No section subtitles that restate the heading. No filler text. If a line can be cut without loss, cut it.
4. **Orange is the eruption.** Use `#f05a28` at full saturation only for moments of genuine emphasis — primary CTAs, accent marks, the CTA section. Diluting it everywhere kills its power.
5. **Sections breathe differently.** Light sections (cream/white) for content and social proof. Dark sections for process and authority. The contrast between them creates rhythm down the page.
